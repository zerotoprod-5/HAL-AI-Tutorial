Numbers · Text · Speech
The one idea behind all of it
Learn the pattern from past examples, then predict the next case. Whether the data was sensor numbers, written reports, or the spoken word — the computer always did the same thing. Not magic, not nonsense: disciplined pattern-finding from records you already keep.

Five words you now own

DatasetA table — rows are cases, columns are measurements.
FeatureAn input column; a clue the model gets to see.
Label columnThe single column holding the answer we predict.
Train / test splitLearn from most rows; judge honestly on hidden ones.
AccuracyHow often it is right on cases it never saw. 100% usually means cheating.
ModelThe pattern-finder. "Training" (.fit) is the moment it learns.

Honest about the limits

Only as good as the data
Biased or thin records give biased or thin predictions.
Never perfect
Real data surprises. We measure the gap, not hide it.
It assists, you decide
It ranks and flags with a reason; the engineer makes the call.
